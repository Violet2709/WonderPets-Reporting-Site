Key Usability Issues Identified

Cancel, back, and edit buttons are not clearly visible or emphasized during the appointment scheduling flow, making it difficult for users to exit or correct mistakes.

Required fields in the appointment form are not marked, and there is no real-time validation to prevent users from submitting incomplete information.

Error messages are not visible or descriptive enough for users to understand what went wrong or how to recover from mistakes.

Button sizes, colors, spacing, and layout elements vary inconsistently between pages, which may confuse users about whether similar elements serve the same function.

Cancel and back buttons lack sufficient visual weight and are not easy to locate, making them easy to overlook during the booking process.

No help center, FAQ section, tooltips, or user guide is available, leaving first-time users without any in-context assistance to navigate the system.

Discussion of Findings

The results of the usability evaluation show that the WonderPets prototype was generally well received by users, with most participants finding the system easy to use, easy to learn, and suitable for frequent use. Users also reported feeling confident while navigating the platform, which indicates that the interface provides enough clarity and guidance for the majority of respondents. In addition, the various functions of the system were perceived as well integrated, reflecting a coherent and consistent design that aligns with Nielsen's heuristic on consistency and standards. The prototype's visual design was frequently praised as clean, intuitive, and pleasing to the eyes — reinforcing that the aesthetic and minimalist design heuristic was effectively applied.

However, some usability issues were still identified during testing, particularly in the areas of error prevention, help and documentation, and user control. Several respondents expressed uncertainty about whether they could navigate the system without technical support — a finding directly tied to the absence of tooltips, a help center, or a FAQ section. This gap was especially noticeable among respondents with no prior experience, who made up 65% of the total participant group. The appointment booking flow also presented friction: cancel and back buttons lacked sufficient visual weight, required fields were not clearly marked, and error messages were not descriptive enough to help users recover from mistakes.

A notable finding was the wide range of individual SUS scores — from a low of 47.5 to a perfect 100 — among a respondent group largely similar in age and academic background. The 52.5-point spread revealed that individual interaction style and prior cognitive models significantly influenced outcomes. Surprisingly, some of the highest scores came from respondents with no prior experience, suggesting the system's visual clarity was immediately comprehensible to certain user types.

Another unanticipated finding was the repeated mention of mobile responsiveness concerns — invisible during the design phase, as the prototype was primarily developed on desktop. Given that 85% of respondents reported daily mobile device use, this gap is significant. These findings collectively show that while the WonderPets prototype performs well in overall usability, improving form design consistency, strengthening error recovery, adding contextual help, and ensuring full mobile responsiveness will meaningfully enhance the next iteration.

Prototype Walkthrough

The Prototype Walkthrough presents the complete user journey of the WonderPets system, showing how users interact with the application from the landing screen up to the final booking confirmation. Each screen was designed to provide a simple, smooth, and user-friendly experience for pet owners. The user flow starts from the Landing Page, followed by the Log In page where users access their accounts. They proceed to the Dashboard, then navigate to the Appointment Booking page to enter their pet's details, and finally select a payment method to complete the booking.

Optional · Reflections

Team Reflections

Personal insights from each group member on the evaluation process, challenges, and learnings.

Conducting the heuristic evaluation for the WonderPets System became a valuable learning experience for me. I learned how important usability is in creating a system that is not only functional but also easy and comfortable for users to navigate. Through the evaluation process, I realized that even small interface details, such as button placement, feedback messages, and consistency of design, can greatly affect the overall user experience. It also helped me understand the importance of applying Nielsen's usability heuristics in identifying possible issues and improving the quality of the system. One of the challenges we encountered was carefully analyzing every part of the system while considering the perspective of different users. Since we wanted the system to be user-friendly for both pet owners and administrators, we had to pay attention to how each feature would be understood and used. Another challenge was organizing our observations and recommendations in a clear and professional manner during the evaluation process. The experience of formulating the results was also challenging because we needed to review the findings carefully, categorize the usability issues properly, and provide realistic recommendations for improvement. It required teamwork, critical thinking, and attention to detail to make sure that the evaluation results were accurate and meaningful.
Rica Rose Vipinoso · Team Leader
During the presentation of our project, we were surprised on the positive feedbacks from our respondents. Although the presentation was done online, most of the respondents were satisfied on the design and functionality of the project, while giving their own insights for minor updates or changes for improvement. What they liked most about the project is the simplicity of the overall layout design and how the interactions and function were implemented.

This project also helped us understand how technology can make services more convenient and accessible to users.
